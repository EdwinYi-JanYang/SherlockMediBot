Sherlock's Mystery Chamber

Case File: The Misinformation Menace

A nefarious individual has been spreading misinformation across the internet. It's up to you, dear Watson, to assist in uncovering the truth.

Suspect: @someDeviousUser

Guys, Malaria is a hoax, I've been to Mexico 5 times and have never caught it

Ah, the case of the missing malarial infection.
I must say, my dear Watson, that this case is a curious one indeed.
The user in question claims that malaria is a hoax, and yet they have been to Mexico five times and have never caught it.
But let us examine the facts.
According to my sources, malaria is a serious and sometimes fatal disease transmitted by mosquitoes.
It cannot be vaccinated against, and precautions such as avoiding mosquito bites and taking antimalarial medication are essential.
Now, let us consider the user's claim.
They have been to Mexico five times and have not caught malaria.
But, my dear Watson, this does not mean that malaria is a hoax.
It is entirely possible that the user has simply been lucky and has not been bitten by an infected mosquito.
Furthermore, my sources indicate that malaria risk in Mexico is low and is present intermittently throughout the year.
In recent years, the states of Campeche, Chiapas, Chihuahua, Durango, Nayarit, Oaxaca, Quintana Roo, Sinaloa, and Tabasco have reported cases.
However, major resorts and the US-Mexico border region are free of the disease.
Therefore, it is entirely possible that the user has visited areas of Mexico where the risk of malaria is low or non-existent.
In conclusion, my dear Watson, while the user's claim that they have not caught malaria in Mexico may be true, it does not prove that malaria is a hoax.
It is a serious and potentially fatal disease, and precautions should always be taken when travelling to areas where it is present.
I give this misinformation an accuracy rating of 0 out of 10.


Watson! It seems our culprit is a serial rabble rouser! We've seen misinformation spread from this individual before!
