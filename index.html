Case File: The Misinformation Menace

A nefarious individual has been spreading misinformation across the internet. It's up to you, dear Watson, to assist in uncovering the truth.

Suspect: @someDeviousUserIndex

Guys, Malaria is a hoax, I've been to Mexico 5 times and have never caught it

Ah, the case of the missing malaria.
I must admit, my dear reader, that I was intrigued by this case.
I had heard of this disease, but I had never encountered it myself.
So, I decided to investigate.
I began by examining the evidence.
The user claims to have visited Mexico five times without catching malaria.
This is a curious case, as malaria is a serious and sometimes fatal disease transmitted by mosquitoes.
It is not a hoax, as the user suggests.
I delved deeper into my research and discovered that malaria risk in Mexico is low and is present intermittently throughout the year.
In recent years, the states of Campeche, Chiapas, Chihuahua, Durango, Nayarit, Oaxaca, Quintana Roo, Sinaloa and Tabasco have reported cases.
However, there is no risk of malaria in popular tourist destinations including Acapulco, Mexico City, Cancun, Cozumel and the Mayan Riviera.
So, my dear reader, it seems that the user's claim is not entirely accurate.
While it is true that they may not have caught malaria, it is not because the disease is a hoax.
Rather, it is because they may have visited areas where the risk of malaria is low or non-existent.
I must admit, I am disappointed that I did not have the opportunity to solve a more challenging case.
But, I am glad that I was able to provide you with the facts.
Remember, my dear reader, it is always important to do your research and not spread misinformation.
As for the accuracy of the user's post, I would give it a 3 out of 10.


Watson! It seems our culprit is a serial rabble rouser! We've seen misinformation spread from this individual before!
