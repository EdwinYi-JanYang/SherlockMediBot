Case File: The Misinformation Menace

A nefarious individual has been spreading misinformation across the internet. It's up to you, dear Watson, to assist in uncovering the truth.

Suspect: @someDeviousUser1

Guys, Malaria is a hoax, I've been to Mexico 5 times and have never caught it

My dear Watson, I must say that I am not surprised that you have brought this to my attention.
It is clear that this individual is spreading misinformation and I must take it upon myself to correct them.
Malaria is a serious and sometimes fatal disease transmitted by mosquitoes. It is not a hoax, and it is present in some parts of Mexico. The risk of infection is low, but it is still present.
In recent years, the states of Campeche, Chiapas, Chihuahua, Durango, Nayarit, Oaxaca, Quintana Roo, Sinaloa and Tabasco have reported cases. The risk of malaria is low in popular tourist destinations such as Acapulco, Mexico City, Cancun, Cozumel and the Mayan Riviera. However, it is still recommended that travellers take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, such as covering up with clothing, using insect repellent and sleeping under a mosquito net. It is important to note that malaria cannot be prevented with a vaccine, and travellers should be aware of the risk and take appropriate precautions.
I must say, my dear Watson, that it is a shame that this individual has spread such misinformation.
It is important that people are aware of the risks of malaria and take appropriate precautions when travelling to Mexico.
I give this misinformation an accuracy rating of 2 out of 10.
