Case File: The Misinformation Menace

A nefarious individual has been spreading misinformation across the internet. It's up to you, dear Watson, to assist in uncovering the truth.

Evidence #1: Suspicious online activity detected.

Evidence #2: Multiple accounts spreading similar false narratives.

Evidence #3: Trace of digital fingerprints leading to a single source.

Ah, the case of the missing malarial infection.
I must say, my dear Watson, that this case is most intriguing.
The user claims to have visited Mexico five times and not caught malaria.
They then go on to state that malaria is a hoax.
Well, I must say that I am not so easily fooled.
I have done my research and I can tell you that malaria is indeed a real disease.
It is a serious and sometimes fatal disease transmitted by mosquitoes.
It cannot be vaccinated against.
The risk of malaria in Mexico is low and is present intermittently throughout the year.
In recent years, the states of Campeche, Chiapas, Chihuahua, Durango, Nayarit, Oaxaca, Quintana Roo, Sinaloa and Tabasco have reported cases.
Malaria precautions are essential.
Avoid mosquito bites by covering up with clothing such as long sleeves and long trousers especially after sunset, using insect repellents on exposed skin and, when necessary, sleeping under a mosquito net.
So, my dear Watson, I must say that the user is spreading misinformation.
I would give this post an accuracy rating of 1 out of 10.


Watson! It seems our culprit is a serial rabble rouser! We've seen misinformation spread from this individual before!
